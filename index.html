SHCLA · Interactive Toolkit

Tools for telling the story of permanent supportive housing

A set of interactive explorers and visual narratives that show who The Union serves, the neighborhood it sits in, and how the SHCLA model differs from the system it replaces. Each opens in your browser, no download needed.

Prepared by Outreach by Design for the Supportive Housing Community Land Alliance

The Model

How the SHCLA model works

Two ways into the theory of change: a side-by-side of the conventional system against the SHCLA model, and the same story told through one resident's path.

The Common System vs the SHCLA Model

An interactive comparison of how the conventional system fails adults with serious mental illness, and how the SHCLA model is structured to hold.

Open

Marcus’s Path

A walk-through of the model through one resident’s journey, showing where the common system breaks down and where SHCLA changes the outcome.

Open
The Population

Who SHCLA serves

Aggregate data on the adults SHCLA is built for: their health profile from the California Health Interview Survey, and their path through Alameda County’s homelessness system.

CHIS Data Explorer

Explore California Health Interview Survey indicators for Alameda County, the health and need profile of the population the model is designed for.

HMIS Data Explorer

Adults in the Homelessness System
